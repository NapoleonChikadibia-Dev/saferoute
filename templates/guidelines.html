Community Guidelines

SafeRoute works because people share honest, useful safety information about places. These guidelines keep it trustworthy — and keep people safe. Reports that break these rules may be hidden or removed.

Report places and incidents — never individuals

SafeRoute is about the safety of locations, not people. Do not post home addresses, full names, phone numbers, license plates, photos of identifiable people, or anything that targets or identifies a specific individual. Even if you believe someone did something wrong, naming or exposing them here is not allowed — it can cause real harm, and it's often defamatory or harassment. Describe what happened and where, not who.

What to report

  • Real safety incidents you witnessed or have good reason to believe happened — theft, robbery, harassment, accidents, unsafe conditions, etc.
  • Conditions that affect safety in an area — poor lighting, flooding, a dangerous junction.
  • Factual, first-hand or well-sourced information that helps others stay safe.

What not to post

  • Anything identifying a specific person — addresses, names, plates, faces. (See above — this is the big one.)
  • False or unverified claims. Don't post rumours, guesses, or things you didn't witness and can't reasonably stand behind. False safety reports can hurt people and mislead the community.
  • Graphic, violent, or disturbing media. Don't upload distressing images or video.
  • Harassment, threats, hate speech, or discrimination of any kind.
  • Spam, advertising, or off-topic content. Keep it about safety.
  • Content that targets a business or person to damage their reputation rather than to inform about genuine safety.

How moderation works

  • Anyone can flag a report they believe is fake or inappropriate. Flagged content is reviewed by our team.
  • Reports that break these guidelines may be hidden from public view. This is reversible — if something was hidden in error, it can be restored.
  • The community can also confirm or dispute reports to signal whether they seem accurate. This is separate from flagging.
  • Repeatedly posting harmful or false content may lead to further action on an account.

A note on accuracy

SafeRoute shows community-reported information and external news for context. It is not a guarantee of safety or danger in any area. Reports reflect what individuals have shared, not a verified or official assessment. Always use your own judgment.
